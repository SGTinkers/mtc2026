## 1. OPEN — The Problem (Desktop)

**Show:** Landing page on desktop

**Say:** Mosques need monthly contributions to stay operational. But recurring donations are much harder to secure than one-off ones. So we built a landing page that storytells the need — showing jemaah how their monthly contribution makes a real difference.

**Do:** Scroll the landing page. Let each section animate in. Don't rush — let it breathe.

---

## 2. THE CHALLENGES

**Say:** The mosque shared three challenge areas with us. Let us show you how we tackled all three.

---

## 3. CHALLENGE 1 — Streamlined Onboarding (Switch to Phone)

> *"How might we design an onboarding experience that works for everyone — from tech-savvy youth to elderly jemaah — and cuts processing time from 21–30 days to under 7?"*

**Say:** The current GIRO process takes 21–30 days. We wanted it done in minutes. And since most users are on mobile, we made it mobile-first.

**Show:** Site on phone

**Do:**
1. Scroll mobile landing page briefly
2. Tap to start giving → choose **$5** (point out how we gently surface the higher plan, but pick $5)
3. Complete payment flow

**Say:** We let the user donate first — secure the subscription before any drop-off. That's the priority. Only then do we get them to complete onboarding by logging in.

**Show:** Login flow → onboarding wizard

**Say:** We know forms are a hassle. So we made the onboarding wizard as joyful as possible. Most fields are prefilled from the device. Done in under 2 minutes. That's the digital-savvy user covered. We'll show how we cater to the elderly and walk-ins shortly in the admin portal.

---

## 4. CHALLENGE 2 — Faster Verification (Stay on Phone)

> *"From 30 days to near-instant — reduce processing from 30 days to under 24 hours."*

**Say:** By replacing GIRO with card-based payments, we bypass the manual bank verification entirely. No GIRO forms, no 30-day wait. Payment is confirmed instantly on signup.

*(This is addressed by the architecture itself — card payment = instant verification. No need for a separate demo, just state it confidently.)*

---

## 5. DASHBOARD — Plan & Impact

**Show:** User dashboard

**Say:** Once onboarded, users see their current plan and how their contribution has grown over time.

---

## 6. WHATSAPP SHARE — Inform Loved Ones

**Show:** WhatsApp CTA button

**Say:** We thought about a real problem — how would my loved ones know I have this benefit if I pass away? One tap sends a WhatsApp message to their next-of-kin, so the family knows they can claim funeral services from the mosque. And it doubles as promotion — their loved ones discover the scheme too.

---

## 7. UPSELL — Family Coverage

**Show:** Upgrade prompt → add family members screen

**Say:** Since we picked $5, we don't get family coverage. So here, we show what's possible and make upgrading seamless. Once upgraded, users can add family members directly.

---

## 8. CHALLENGE 3 — Proactive Member Care: Failed Payment Recovery

> *"How might we prevent members from losing coverage without warning? Reduce membership lapses by 80%."*

**Show:** Subscription card / payment update page

**Say:** Previously with GIRO, if the card had insufficient balance, after 3 failed attempts it just gets cancelled silently. Now, the user receives an email with a link to their payment page — where they can downgrade to a lower plan or update their card. No silent cancellations, no lost coverage.

---

## 9. ADMIN PORTAL (Switch to Desktop)

**Say:** Now let's look at the admin side — what the mosque staff sees.

### Dashboard
**Show:** Admin dashboard

**Say:** Staff see the numbers that matter: current monthly recurring donations, new donors this month, uptake trends, breakdown by scheme.

### Failed Payment Management (Challenge 3 continued)
**Show:** Failed payments view → list of affected users

**Say:** Directly addressing Challenge 3 — admins can see members with failed payments. They can choose to nudge those users to recover, or observe it as a trend to monitor churn rate.

### Manual Onboarding (Challenge 1 continued)
**Show:** Admin onboarding wizard + OCR

**Say:** Challenge 1 asked how we design for everyone — from tech-savvy youth to elderly jemaah. For those who aren't comfortable online, they can walk in and staff can either guide them through the Stripe payment flow on the spot, or if they prefer cash and GIRO, we cater to that too. Staff get a smooth onboarding wizard, plus OCR that auto-fills the form from the user's handwritten paper form. So no one is left behind.

### Claims Records
**Show:** Claims management screen

**Say:** Admins can handle and store claims records. This gives insights into whether the plan is delivering the intended benefit. It also serves as a way to activate jemaah — for example, if there's a tahlil event, staff can see members who've recently had family pass away and reach out to invite them.

## 10. CLOSE — Future Ideas

**Say:** Looking ahead, we want users to browse eligible courses and claim their discounts directly from the app. This improves discoverability of the mosque's programmes and gives members real satisfaction in using the perks they've earned.

**Tips:**
- Keep transitions snappy between screens
- Story arc: Problem → Challenges → Demo (user) → Demo (admin) → Future
- Emotion first, product second
- Explicitly name each challenge area before demoing the solution
- Perks are cherry on top, not the main pitch
